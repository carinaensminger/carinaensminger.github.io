bluevogel

ux|ui design

As one of Yelling Mule's Web Designers, I had the pleasure of designing a software program for one of our clients. During this project I lead both visual and UX design. I was responsible for creating the information architecture, wireframes, high fidelity mock-ups, and clickable prototype for this project. Blue Vogel is a small SAAS start-up focused on helping sales reps reach a large number of cold email prospects with messages individualized to the recipients' job title and industry. The product was in its infancy stages with few features or flows fleshed out.

knowing the user...

I began the project by speaking with 5 sales representatives about their sales cycle and the way in which they go about cold emailing individuals. It was crucial for the user to be able to differentiate their messages by the recipient's line of business (ie. the type of role in their company) and their industry. Users also wanted to control their message as much as possible.

...designing for their needs

To account for these two desires, I divided the Inputs section into two parts and designed these as paragraph inputs. When a user uploads a spreadsheet of cold contacts, the system then finds their line of business and their industry and matches them with the correct inputs.

... and for business goals

The prototype to the left depicts all of the features and functionality of the general system and also integrates the permissions & flows of the system based upon the user's account. The pricing model of the platform was threefold: free version, basic version, and professional version. Each version had more features and more functionality. This prototype assumes that the user has signed up for a free account.
