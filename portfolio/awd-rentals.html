AWD Rentals

Back to Portfolio Next Project

As Yelling Mule’s Junior Web Designer, I designed the booking flow for one of our client’s AWD Rentals’ booking website. The color and font choices were chosen by our head designer. Everything else I led the design of.

research

I worked with both the team at AWD Rentals and Yelling Mule to understand the constraints of their business and the system we were working with. As a fledgling company, AWD Rentals' greatest bottleneck was the size of their fleet. With only 6 cars in total it was crucial that we designed a flow that could easily allow users to know whether or not the car they've selected was available.

The system we were using to book the products also presented some challenges. The backend system our client used was originally made for online shopping, not booking. The challenge therefore, was how to redesign and tweak these preset templates to fit a booking flow rather than a shopping flow.

I also did quick research what factors people who are renting cars consider important in making their decision- things like mileage, trunk space, and amount of seats were very important to users.

ideate

During the ideation process, I sketched out multiple possible flows that would work with both within the constraints of the templates we were using and would make sense as a booking flow.

iterate

Once we spoke with the client and okayed the user flows, I began on the visual design. Ideally, I would have tested the wireframes, but unfortunately the time constraints of the project did not allow us to do so.

The head designer of Yelling Mule chose font and color schemes for the project. The layout, and organization of information on every page was determined by me. I chose to go with a clean layout with bright red buttons to help bring the user through the process quickly. I integrated icons that presented information about the car (mileage, seats etc.) to help highlight those things that users found important when looking for a car to book.

All design work was done in photoshop. The final clickable prototype was done using Invisionapp.

refine

I am currently refining this project in my spare time as a thought project. I wanted to create a booking flow divorced from the backend constraints of the system but still with the constraints of few resources within the fleet in an attempt to find a better design. I am beginning this process again with deeper user interviews, and observational research and plan to user test wireframes before deciding upon a flow.
